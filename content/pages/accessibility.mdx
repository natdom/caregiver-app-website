# Accessibility Statement

*Last updated: January 1, 2024*

withCare is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all users, including caregivers with disabilities. We strive to provide an inclusive experience that allows everyone to access our resources and support.

## Our Commitment

We are dedicated to providing a website that is accessible to the widest possible audience, regardless of ability or technology. We continually improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards.

## Accessibility Standards

We aim to conform to the [Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/quickref/) Level AA standards. These guidelines help make web content more accessible for people with disabilities and user-friendly for everyone.

## Current Accessibility Features

Our website includes:

### Navigation and Structure
- Logical heading structure (H1, H2, H3, etc.)
- Skip navigation links for keyboard users
- Consistent navigation throughout the site
- Clear page titles and descriptions

### Visual Design
- High contrast color combinations that meet WCAG AA standards
- Text that can be resized up to 200% without loss of functionality
- Clear visual focus indicators for keyboard navigation
- Alternative text for all meaningful images

### Interaction
- All functionality available via keyboard
- Form labels clearly associated with inputs
- Error messages that are clear and helpful
- Sufficient time limits with options to extend

### Content
- Plain language whenever possible
- Clear link text that describes the destination
- Content structure that works with screen readers
- Video content includes captions (when applicable)

## Assistive Technology Support

Our website is designed to work with:
- Screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
- Voice recognition software
- Keyboard navigation
- Switch navigation
- Screen magnification software

## Ongoing Improvements

We are continuously working to improve accessibility:
- Regular accessibility audits and testing
- User testing with assistive technology users
- Staff training on accessibility best practices
- Updates based on user feedback

## Accessibility Feedback

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of withCare. If you encounter accessibility barriers or have suggestions for improvement:

### Contact Us
- **Email**: accessibility@support.network
- **Subject Line**: Please use "Accessibility" in the subject line
- **Information to Include**:
  - Description of the issue
  - Page or section where you encountered the problem
  - Assistive technology you were using (if applicable)
  - Your contact information for follow-up

### Response Time
We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 2 business days and will work with you to address any issues promptly.

## Known Limitations

We are aware of the following accessibility limitations and are working to address them:
- Some third-party embedded content may not be fully accessible
- PDF documents are being updated to meet accessibility standards
- Some older content is being reviewed and updated for accessibility

## Alternative Formats

If you need information from our website in an alternative format:
- Large print
- Audio format
- Plain text email
- Other accessible formats

Please contact us at accessibility@support.network, and we will work to provide the information in your preferred format.

## Accessibility Statement for Mobile Apps

When our mobile applications launch, they will be designed with accessibility in mind, including:
- VoiceOver and TalkBack support
- Large text support
- High contrast mode compatibility
- Voice control functionality

## Third-Party Content

While we strive to ensure accessibility across our entire website, some third-party content (embedded videos, social media, etc.) may not meet our accessibility standards. We work with vendors to improve accessibility and provide alternative access methods when possible.

## Training and Awareness

Our team receives regular training on:
- Web accessibility principles
- Inclusive design practices
- Assistive technology awareness
- Content creation for accessibility

## Legal Compliance

We are committed to compliance with applicable accessibility laws, including:
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act
- State and local accessibility requirements

## Regular Reviews

This accessibility statement is reviewed and updated:
- Annually or when significant changes are made to the website
- Following accessibility audits
- Based on user feedback and changing standards
